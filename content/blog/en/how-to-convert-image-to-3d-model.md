## Pro Tips for Exceptional Results

### Mastering Image Preparation

**Tip 1: The Background Removal Technique**
Before uploading, consider removing the background from your image. This helps the AI focus entirely on your subject without confusion from surrounding elements.

**Tip 2: Lighting Consistency**
If possible, photograph your subject with soft, even lighting from multiple angles. Avoid direct sunlight which creates harsh shadows that can confuse depth estimation.

**Tip 3: Multiple Angle Strategy**
For complex objects, generate models from multiple angles and compare results. Sometimes a different perspective yields better geometry.

### Post-Processing Recommendations

Once you've downloaded your model, consider these enhancements:

1. **Mesh Cleanup** - Use Blender's decimate modifier to reduce polygon count if needed
2. **UV Refinement** - Adjust UV mapping for better texture alignment
3. **Material Enhancement** - Add PBR materials for realistic rendering
4. **Scale Adjustment** - Ensure proper real-world scale for your application

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Problem: Model Has Holes or Missing Parts

**Solutions:**
- Use a higher resolution source image
- Ensure the entire object is visible in the frame
- Try a different angle that shows the missing area
- Check that lighting reveals all surface details

### Problem: Textures Look Blurry

**Solutions:**
- Start with a higher resolution image (2048√ó2048 recommended)
- Ensure the source image is in focus
- Avoid heavily compressed JPEGs
- Use PNG format for best quality

### Problem: Geometry Is Distorted

**Solutions:**
- Simplify the background
- Use a more straightforward angle
- Ensure good contrast between subject and background
- Try the "Characters" mode for organic shapes
